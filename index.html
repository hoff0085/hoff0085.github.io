What is a Quokka?

A Quokka is a small macropod marsupial native to South Western Australia. Quokkas are small in size and are very friendly. Currently, the Quokka is classified as vulnerable by the IUCN Red List of Threatened Species.





Where do Quokkas live?

Quokkas are found in south-western Western Australia and the small coastal islands in the area. On the coastal islands, Quokkas number in the thousands, but on the mainland it is difficult to find the small marsupials outside of sanctuaries.
